Top 5 of The Most Beautiful Waterfalls in The World

When life becomes overwhelming, one thing you can do is watch waterfall videos. I have located some information from various sources for the above Ten Most Beautiful Waterfalls in The World. This information is in addition to what is presented during the video presentation.


The top 5 are as follows:

  1. Niagara Falls is known as the most famous waterfall in the world and is comprised of three waterfalls. The Horseshoe Falls/Canadian Falls is the largest. There is the American Falls, and then the smallest – the Bridal Veil Falls.
  2. Skogafoss Waterfall in South Ireland is stated as being stunning and even has the option to climb stairs to reach the top of the waterfall.
  3. Angel Falls located in Venezuela, currently holds a 5-star rating out of 689 reviews on the Trip Advisor site. Many sources state that this is the tallest waterfall in the world at over 900 meters, including the visual located on this site.
  4. Iguazu Falls is shared between Argentina and Brazil. There is no swimming permitted but boat rides are permitted.
  5. Victoria Falls is located near Zambia and Zimbabwe; this waterfall is in existence because of the Zambezi River and was first discovered in 1855.

The Remaining 5 of The Most Beautiful Waterfalls in The World:

Now that we have had time to process the top 5, let’s take a moment and appreciate the remainder of the Most Beautiful Waterfalls in the World. I'm sure we can all concur that these are very attractive waterfalls.

  1. Gullfoss Waterfall is known by many as the “Golden Falls” because it changes to a golden-brown color during sunny days.
  2. Yosemite Falls, California has its water flow peak especially in the late spring and is known as the highest waterfall in Yosemite National Park at 2,425 feet as reiterated from the visual.
  3. Seljalandsfoss Falls is known as being the only waterfall in Iceland that people can safely walk behind as explained in the video.
  4. Plitvice Falls is located within Plitvice Lakes National Park. The park itself occupies 195 KM of lakes, meadows, and forests with hiking trails along the waterfalls and lakes.
  5. Sutherland Falls, New Zealand is believed to be amongst the tallest waterfalls at over 500 meters high.
